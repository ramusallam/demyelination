Neural Degradation

A Multiple Sclerosis Simulation

1. Exploration

Observe the action potential (the glowing signal) traveling down the healthy axon. It is wrapped in myelin sheaths (the yellow segments).

Notice how the signal jumps rapidly between the gaps. This hyper-fast travel is called saltatory conduction.

2. Hypothesis

Multiple Sclerosis (MS) is an autoimmune disease where the body's immune system attacks and destroys the myelin sheaths.

Question: What do you predict will happen to the speed of the action potential if myelin is removed?

3. Intervention

Your cursor is now simulating an autoimmune attack. Click on the myelin sheaths on the axon to destroy them.

Observe the changes in signal speed, and watch what happens to the brain MRI view below as myelin is lost (demyelination).

4. Conclusion

Without myelin insulation, neural signals degrade and slow down drastically. On the MRI scan, we see this damage as white matter lesions (bright spots).

These lesions represent areas of demyelination and inflammation, leading to the sensory and motor issues characteristic of MS.
